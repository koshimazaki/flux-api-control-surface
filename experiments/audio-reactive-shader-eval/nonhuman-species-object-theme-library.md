# Non-Human Species, Object, And Theme Library

Reusable source library for audio-reactive / video-guided non-human video tests.

Use one item from each group:

1. Species or object subject
2. Motion archetype
3. Environment / theme
4. Transition target

Keep prompts original. For public demos, avoid naming specific franchises directly; translate them into genre ingredients like "mythic space opera salvage" or "ancient puzzle-temple futurism."

## Species Families

### 1. Abyssal Coral Servo Colony

- Visual: bioluminescent coral polyps, glass coral branches, sea-anemone crowns, tube-worm filaments, chrome hydraulic joints.
- Motion: polyps open/close to bass, coral fans unfurl, branches pulse outward then recoil, whole colony rotates like a slow mechanical gyroscope.
- Best for: underwater shader tests, radial expansion, branching morphs, glowing fractal transitions.
- Transition target: glowing coral core -> mechanical flower -> crystalline reef organism.

### 2. Kelp Ribbon Engine

- Visual: dark green kelp ribbons, translucent fins, wet carbon fiber veins, tiny servo hinges along the edges.
- Motion: ribbons snap, wave, twist, braid, and unbraid in sync with impacts.
- Best for: flowing motion, audio swells, shader-to-organism translation.
- Transition target: kelp ribbons -> heliconia-like petals -> turbine fan organism.

### 3. Lithops Shrine Bloom

- Visual: living-stone lithops, black pitcher-plant mouths, passion-flower tendrils, wet basalt altar.
- Motion: stone plates split open, tendrils breathe, central crown spins, petals fold inward like an iris.
- Best for: current passion-flower set, dark botanical-mechanical mood.
- Transition target: light flower -> dark flower -> hybrid flower.

### 4. Mycelium Antenna Crown

- Visual: mushroom gills, fungal threads, porcelain caps, copper antenna filaments, glowing spores.
- Motion: gills ripple, spore sacs pulse, antennae extend/retract, caps open like satellite dishes.
- Best for: soft organic motion with readable beat pulses.
- Transition target: mushroom cap -> antenna array -> luminous spore reactor.

### 5. Geode Bloom Machine

- Visual: cracked mineral shell, quartz petals, opal membranes, metallic root struts.
- Motion: crystal plates slide, facets rotate, inner light pulses, shell opens into a flower-like geode.
- Best for: sharpness/coherence tests and non-fluid mechanical transitions.
- Transition target: closed geode -> crystal flower -> glass coral.

### 6. Seed Pod Iris

- Visual: pinecone scales, lotus pod holes, brushed titanium ribs, seed chambers, amber sap.
- Motion: scales lift one by one, holes glow, seed chambers inflate/deflate, pod twists open.
- Best for: opening/closing LoRA material and product-like macro realism.
- Transition target: seed pod -> mechanical iris -> botanical speaker.

### 7. Ceramic Shell Resonator

- Visual: nautilus-shell spiral, porcelain glaze, mother-of-pearl, brass pins, silicone membranes.
- Motion: spiral chambers pulse, shell aperture opens/closes, membranes vibrate visibly to bass.
- Best for: musical-object hybrids without using humans or animals as characters.
- Transition target: shell -> horn speaker -> coral instrument.

### 8. Inflatable Botanical Toy

- Visual: glossy vinyl petals, soft balloon membranes, toy-like seams, embedded micro-servos, translucent gel core.
- Motion: inflates, deflates, bounces, twists, then snaps into crisp mechanical folds.
- Best for: exaggerated audio-reactive motion and playful product tests.
- Transition target: toy flower -> soft robot -> luxury object bloom.

### 9. Liquid-Metal Pollen Organ

- Visual: mercury-like droplets, black chrome petals, magnetic pollen beads, transparent membranes.
- Motion: droplets gather and scatter on impacts, petals ripple, core liquefies then re-forms.
- Best for: shader-like movement translated into physical macro matter.
- Transition target: liquid metal pool -> flower core -> mechanical species.

### 10. Paper-Lantern Biomech

- Visual: washi paper folds, bamboo-like ribs, glowing internal core, wet silk membranes, tiny robotic pivots.
- Motion: lantern expands/contracts, ribs fan open, paper skin ripples to rhythm.
- Best for: elegant, readable non-human motion with strong silhouette.
- Transition target: closed lantern -> flower lantern -> floating shrine mechanism.

## Object Families

- Perfume bottle that opens into a mechanical flower.
- Fountain pen nib splitting into radial filaments.
- Camera lens iris growing botanical tendrils.
- Watch mechanism blooming into metallic petals.
- Speaker pod whose membranes become coral polyps.

- Chess piece / ritual totem that opens into a luminous core.
- Luxury lighter / capsule / compact mirror with botanical internals.
- Toy capsule inflating into a soft robotic plant.
- Glass paperweight becoming a geode organism.

### Mundane Product Objects

- Perfume bottle: cap/pump opens into radial petals, atomizer mist pulses to bass.
- Fountain pen: nib splits into filaments, ink channel glows, barrel telescopes open.
- Camera lens: aperture breathes open/closed, focus rings rotate, glass core blooms.
- Wristwatch: gears pulse, case opens into metal petals, dial compresses into a glowing seed.
- Headphones: ear cups open like shells, speaker mesh vibrates, headband flexes.
- Sneaker: sole inflates/deflates, side ribs open, laces become soft robotic tendrils.
- Compact car model: hood/doors pulse open, wheel hubs spin, panels unfold into fins.
- Car key fob: shell splits open, inner ribs pulse, small glowing pod emerges.
- Coffee cup: liquid surface pulses, steam spirals, rim opens into a soft mechanical iris.
- Desk lamp: lamp head opens into radial petals, arm pulses like a servo, light breathes.
- Speaker pod: grille vibrates, coral-like membranes grow from the face.
- Smartphone: glass ripples, screen panels split, luminous geometry opens from the center.

For product use cases, keep the object unbranded, centered, and recognizable before transformation. The sales story is stronger when the viewer can immediately read the starting object.

### Material-First Objects

- Porcelain shell -> brass hinge -> translucent organic tissue.
- Carbon fiber petal -> jelly membrane -> chrome filament.
- Amber resin pod -> seed chamber -> light-emitting tendrils.
- Opal crystal -> liquid metal -> coral skeleton.
- Wax seal -> mechanical iris -> botanical engine.

## Motion Archetypes

- Bass snap: closed object opens instantly on the first impact.
- Breathing pulse: subject expands and contracts once per beat.
- Servo spin: slow 360-degree mechanical rotation with tiny lock-in steps.
- Iris fold: petals/ribs fold inward like a camera aperture.
- Hydraulic bloom: plates slide outward with piston-like force.
- Tendril recoil: filaments stretch outward, then whip back into the core.
- Core compression: whole subject collapses into a dense glowing seed.
- Branching growth: structures split into smaller filaments on each impact.
- Membrane vibration: soft surfaces visibly tremble to bass.
- Spiral twist: subject corkscrews open while the camera pushes in.

### LoRA Material Set

Aim for 20-30 short clips or image/video pairs:

- 5 opening/closing botanical mechanisms.
- 5 underwater coral / kelp organisms.
- 5 product-object transformations.
- 5 mineral / geode / liquid-metal organisms.
- 5 optional soft robotics / toy-like membrane subjects.

Keep repeated structure across the set: centered macro subject, one organism/object, clear open state, clear closed/compressed state, strong radial or branching motion, no humans, no separate collage elements.

## Compact Prompt Formula

```text
9s cinematic top-down macro video, one non-human [subject]. Use @vid1 for motion rhythm and camera/action reference, not visual style. Start as @img1, morph through @img2, end as @img3.

[environment/theme]. The subject [motion archetype 1], [motion archetype 2], and [motion archetype 3] to the beat. Midway it compresses into a glowing core, then opens into the final form as one stable organism.

Photorealistic macro VFX, Fuji film look, shallow DOF, wet highlights, natural motion blur, stable subject continuity. No humans, text, UI, collage, duplicate subjects, abstract rings/bars/metronome, flat shader look.
```
